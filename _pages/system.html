Concept

N.E.A.T. is an assistive relay robot designed for those with mobility issues which impede their ability to carry items as they walk. Our key target markets are those with mobility impairments and the elderly. Having designed N.E.A.T. with this target market in mind, its key features include a simple and accessible mobile application which is used to control the robot, two different movement options: "follow me" and manually controlled movement, and a scissor lift which offers various heights for ease of use.

The N.E.A.T. robot consists of a Lego base and integrated scissor lift with a non-slip tray atop it. The lift allows N.E.A.T. to raise this tray up to 91cm above the ground, and can support up to 3kg of weight. This means your N.E.A.T. is ideal for carrying items such as your meal from the kitchen to the dining table, your books from your study to the living room or your laptop from your bedroom to your desk.

Your N.E.A.T. offers two different kinds of movement. The first is standard manual control of the robot, which allows you to move it in eight directions and rotate clockwise and anticlockwise. The second is one of the things which makes N.E.A.T. so useful: the "follow me" feature, which uses an infrared sensor and beacon to follow a user to their destination. Both types of movement and the lift are controlled by the N.E.A.T. Android application, which is designed to comply with W3C accessibility guidelines and allows a user to control their N.E.A.T. using either buttons or voice commands.

Market Research

Mobility impaired people requiring assistance

Study has shown that mobility disability, defined as “when impairments in mobility restrict the ability of individuals to move about in their natural environment in order to carry out activities essential to daily life”, can significantly reduce people's quality of life including physical, mental and cognitive health. There are over 11 million people in the UK alone with a limiting long term illness, impairment or disability. Among them, the majority are above 65 and the most common causes are arthritis, rheumatism and back or spine problems, in total accounting for 40%. One of the major issues for these individuals is moving while carrying objects. Elderly people, especially those living alone have a difficult time in their living environment. According to the national survey , more than 70% of people believe that older people require support for them to live independently at home and one in five disabled people find adaptations made to their accommodation unsuitable or unsatisfactory. Therefore, our product, which aims to allow the user to have more control within their own home by easing their dependence on assistive living and the need for home adaptation, has a huge potential market.

Compare with similar products

Currently, there are three types of robots in the market similar to ours: the restaurant server robot, the outdoor food delivery robot and the cargo carrying robot. Each of them shares a few similarities with our robot, but none of them simultaneously possess all the necessary properties for a home use carrying robot for mobility impaired people. As a non-robotic alternative, those who rely on a walker for assistance can use a walker with a built-in tray, but once again, N.E.A.T. offers unique advantages over this option.

Alibaba's food waiter robot
Alibaba's new restaurants use robot waiters to serve their customers. It also targets indoor food transmitting. However, because it is designed for restaurants, this robot is not qualified for our use case. In order to effectively navigate to customers, it requires a specialised track to operate on, which it is not feasible to build for the majority of homes.
Bear Robotics' waiter robot
This robot waiter is more flexible in terms of mobility and shares a similar outlook as ours. The major advantage of our robot is supporting human following and custimized robot configuration i.e. user can adjust tray's height when placing/removing food.
On the contrary, the robot waiter only has a fixed height to place the food, making it harder for customers to move the food to irregular table. Hence, the fact that our robot places food on a lift enables the robot to deal with this variation.
Food delivery robots
Food delivery robots are able to transmit packaged food from restaurants to customers. They are designed for food delivery with a lot of extra features to guarantee food safety, resulting in much higher price for each robot.
Also, food delivery robot is supposed to work with a number of similar robots collaboratively, which requires a large central server to schedule each robot’s task. Neither is there a way for users to customize or manipulate each robot independently nor it is suitable for personal use.
Gita cargo carrying robot
This cargo carrying robot can follow the user while moving. It keeps track of user's moving trajectory by matching its own camera’s video with the input from a camera belt wearing by the user. On the other hand, our robot traces the infrared beacon’s signal held by the user, which means users don’t need to purchase any additional wearable devices and further developments may even remove this requirement by implementing object-localisation based on camera video. Also, the user has to bend over to get their stuff from this robot, which is inconvenient for our robot’s target audience - people with mobility impairments.
Assistive walker with tray
Some assistive walkers provide a built-in tray which can be used to carry items while the user is walking. Although this is a viable option for those who use a walker, N.E.A.T. appeals to a larger target market than simply those with a walker. Additional advantages of N.E.A.T. include the increased stability and safety offered by an independent robot rather than relying on the balance of the person using the walker. Another key feature is the scissor lift offered by the N.E.A.T. This allows users to place items on the tray and remove them with minimum effort, as they can avoid bending or stretching.

Integrated with smart home system, a brand new market

The potential of embedding the N.E.A.T robot into a smart home system makes our robot unique in the market. In the past few years, there has been an increasing trend in development of smart home devices and personal assistant robots, especially for the old or mobility impaired groups. These people usually experience dramatic hardship in accomplishing daily tasks like holding stuff firmly while walking. On the other hand, smart home devices are “easy-to-use” and aim for automatic living experience which is more accessible to the general public. Study has been done in investigating the potential application of combining robotics and smart home IoT. and exploring building assistant robots in a dynamic smart environment. Companies have already developed a number of smart home devices for old people including smart locks, smart Lighting, automated Thermostats, and Leak Detection. Despite the increasing popularity of smart home devices, there isn’t any commercial product that integrates good carrying robots into a smart home ecosystem. Therefore, our product, N.E.A.T. is capable of opening a new market area.

Potential for future development

  • Augment the Android app with WiFi localization for better user experience. It enables the robot to localise the user within the room when it is woken up from idle mode. Therefore, the user can place the robot anywhere in the house for charging or placement. When the user want to use the robot, he or she can invoke the robot while preparing food. Then the robot will navigate itself to the user and be ready for carrying food after a while.
  • Implement computer vision algorithms for object detection and recognition in the future.
    There are two potential benefits:
    1. First, we can fully get rid of the infrared beacon when following and give the user more freedom when using our robot. Deep learning algorithms will be integrated into our system for object localisation, i,e. identify an object and isolate it from the rest of objects including background within an image. Thus, the robot is able to follow the user based on analysing the image from the camera.
    2. In addition, adding camera based object detection allows the system to support indoor mapping. In combination with WiFi localization on the app, the robot is able to come to the user from any place in the house.
  • Implementation of battery lifetime notifications that allow the user to get a time estimate of the remaining time (not just a percentage) that the robot can work for before requiring charged
  • Implementation of a “home docking system” that would charge the robot during downtime with minimal human interaction
  • Integrate the robot with existing home automation devices together to enhance the smart home ecosystem. Currently, the mobile phone has to be in the same local network to connect to the robot. With the help of other smart home devices, the user can remotely control the robot. It can then extend to more complicated use cases beyond just carrying food between kitchen and dining room. For instance,imagine the user is heading back home from shopping, the user can ask the robot to come to the door by talking to a smart home interface installed on his or her mobile phone when he or she finishes shopping. The command will be propagated to the robot in the house by the central hub that connects to the internet. When the user arrives at home, the robot will be in front of them ready to carry stuffs.
