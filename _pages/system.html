Market research

Mobile impaired people demanding assistant

Study has shown that mobility disability, defined as “when impairments in mobility restrict the ability of individuals to move about in their natural environment in order to carry out activities essential to daily life”, can significantly impair people's quality of life including physical, mental and cognitive health. There are over 11 million people in the UK alone with a limiting long term illness, impairment or disability. Among them, majority are above 65 and the most common causes are arthritis, rheumatism and back or spine problems, in total accounting for 40%. One of the major issues for these individuals is moving while carrying objects. Elderly people, especially those living alone have a difficult time in their living environment. According to the national survey , more than 70% of people believe that older people demand support for them to live independently at home and one in five disabled people find adaptations made to their accommodation unsuitable or unsatisfactory. Therefore, our product, aiming to allow the user to have more control within their own home by easing their dependence on assistive living and the need for home adaptation, has a huge potential market.

Compare with similar products

Currently, there are three types of robots in the market similar to ours: the restaurant server robot, the outdoor food delivery robot and the cargo carrying robot. Each of them shares a few similarities with our robot, but none of them simultaneously possess all the necessary properties for a home use carrying robot for mobility impaired people.
Alibaba's food waiter robot
Alibaba's new restaurants use robot waiter to serve their customers. It also target for indoor food transmitting. However, because it is designed for restaurants, this robot is not qualified for our use case. In order to effectively navigate to customers, it requires a specialised track to operate on, which it is not feasible to build for majority of home.
Bear Robotics' waiter robot
This robot waiter is more flexible in terms of mobility and shares a similar outlook as ours. The major advantage of our robot is supporting human following and custimized robot configuration i.e. user can adjust tray's height when placing/removing food. On the contrary, the robot waiter only has a fixed height to place the food, making it harder for customers to move the food to irregular table. Hence, the fact that our robot places food on a lift enables the robot to deal with this variation.
Food delivery robots
Food delivery robots are able to transmit packaged food from restaurants to customers. They are designed for food delivery with a lot of extra features to guarantee food safety, resulting in much higher price for each robot. Also, its colony working mode, i.e. a number of robots collaborate with each other, requires a large central server to schedule each robot’s task. Neither is there a way for users to customize or manipulate nor is the robot suitable for personal use.
Gita cargo carrying robot
This cargo carrying robot can follow the user while moving. It keeps track of user's moving trajectory by . On the other hand, our robot traces the infrared beacon’s signal holding by the user, which means users don’t need to purchase any additional wearable devices and further development may even remove this requirement by implementing object-localisation based on camera video. Also, the user has to bend over to get their stuff from this robot, which is inconvenient for our robot’s target audience-people with mobility impairments.
